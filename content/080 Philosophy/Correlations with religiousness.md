Commonly cited finding that people who are religious tend to live longer and experience other health benefits over other demographics. 


Summarizing [pew research study](https://www.pewresearch.org/religion/2019/01/31/religions-relationship-to-happiness-civic-engagement-and-health-around-the-world/#religion-health-link) shows 
- Religion typically correlates with 
	1) not smoking tobacco
	2) consistently voting
	3) greater self-perceived happiness
	4) non-religious community activity
	5) less drinking
	compared to normal population
- Religion does not correlate significantly with 
	6) better self-rated health
	7) regular exercise
	8) lower obesity
- *speaking generally* other demographic factors like sex, age, and marital status have far stronger implications on the 8 factors discussed above
