# Definitions and Verbiage
> [!info]
> **Gross Vehicle Weight Rating** (GVWR)
> is the maximum total weight the vehicle can safely operate at. This includes the vehicle's own weight, passengers, cargo, and *tongue weight* (not necessarily trailer weight)

> [!info]
> **Total Load Capacity** (TLC)
> is the weight the vehicle can safely move. This includes ~~the vehicle's own weight~~, passengers, cargo, and *tongue weight* (not necessarily trailer weight)

> [!info]
> **Tongue Weight** 
> is the amount of weight from the trailer. Tongue weight *should* be about 10-15% of the total trailer's weight (including itself and any cargo). This needs to be measured and adjusted manually

> [!info]
> **Towing Capacity** 
> is the maximum gross trailer weight (trailer itself plus any cargo) the vehicle is able to tow. This is a function of how quickly your engine and breaks can accelerate
# Relevant Data
For my specific Toyota 2006 Highlander:
- GVWR: 5,360 lbs
- Towing Capacity: 3,500 lbs
- Total Load Cap: 1,159 lbs
- Tongue Weight: must be measured at loading
- Hitch Max Tongue Weight: 350 lbs
- Ball Mount Max weight: 6,000 lbs
