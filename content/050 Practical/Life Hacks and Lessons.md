> Notes to myself about things which seem like they'd be useful, but perhaps not for a while. Or maybe just little tips and tricks you'd like to put together to pass on to the next generation. 



### Grab Bag!
- You can by a trust to hold your land, which anonymizes your name. Costs like 200$
- Often times gas stations will charge the "credit" rate for debit purchases. Pay cash instead.
- Save job description, pictures, and redacted company presentation before you exit. It will help with demonstrating capabilities to the next employer


### Moving
- Assume the people handling your items are actual gorillas. They truly don't care and will throw things any which way

### Travel
- Truck stops will let you sleep in the parking lot, even if you are not a trucker. However they got a little touchy when I tried to reside in one of the trucking spots despite being there hours before the lot filled up. 
- If you are going to sleep in your car: pillow, camping pad, towels to cover the windows/sunshade. Air flow is challenging, particularly in summer weather, so hopefully you can crack the sunroof. 
- The EU multi-day rail passes are generally not a better deal than simply booking the tickets directly. So if you can afford to plan, it's more economical.
